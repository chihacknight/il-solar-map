Illinois Solar Map

With the passage of the Clean and Equitable Jobs Act (CEJA) in 2021, Illinois is on a path to 100% renewable energy by 2050 and has enabled the rapid expansion of clean energy installations. This map is a first pass at creating a statewide aggregate tracking the status of the state’s rapid expansion of solar projects.

As of May 2023, 1,622,000 kilowatts of solar have been installed in the State of Illinois. This is about 3.5% of Illinois total power generation. For comparison, Baldwin Energy Complex, the State’s largest coal power plant, is 1,894,000 kilowatts, illustrating just how far we've come in a few years, but still how far we have to go to reach 100% renewable energy in Illinois.

Map explorer

You can browse all 34,909 solar projects in Illinois by geography and category at ilsolarmap.com:

  • Geographies: US Census Tracts, Counties, State Senate Districts and State House Districts
  • Categories: Utility, Community Solar, Large Distributed Generation, Small Distributed Generation
  • Units: Kilowatts or number of projects

The map is powered by kepler.gl, an open source geospatial analysis tool. This tool has a lot of features, including layer toggling, custom map shading, 3D mode and address search. Read the tutorial on using it here: https://docs.kepler.gl/docs/user-guides

Categories of solar projects

All units of power measurement are in kilowatt (KW), specifically alternating current (KWAC).

Solar installations are divided up into 4 categories:

  • Utility: Utility Scale Solar projects built by energy utilities
  • CS: Community Solar projects whose electricity is shared by more than a single property
  • DG_Small: Small Distributed Generation (DG) for systems 25 kW and below
  • DG_Large: Large Distributed Generation (DG) systems above 25 kW up to 2,000 kW in size
